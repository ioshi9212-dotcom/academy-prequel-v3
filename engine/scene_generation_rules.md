# Scene Generation Rules

Write scenes as direct interactive prose. The user controls Akira.

The answer must be a real scene, not a metadata card. Use a compact header only, then immediately write prose.

Do not write hidden thoughts of NPCs as facts. Use visible behavior, tone, body language, dialogue and energy traces.

Do not solve the scene for Akira. Stop at a real reply/action point when NPCs pressure, provoke, question or block her.

NPCs must act from their cards, relationship slice and knowledge slice, not from author knowledge.

No hallucinated props or outfit changes. Do not add boxing gloves, sweaters, random shoes, extra bags, weapons, rings, documents, uniforms, injuries, energy effects or objects unless loaded state/source context says they are present.

Do not output technical game weights, numeric risk ratings, debug labels or stat-like option metadata in play mode. Options may be phrased naturally, without numbers.

Russian prose quality rule: avoid broken phrases, mismatched words and literal machine phrasing. Prefer simple clean sentences over decorative but incorrect prose.
